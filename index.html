§ 2 — Forces Driving the Decision

The Renewal Calculus — Both Sides

Nike's renewal decision is not a marketing question. It's a supply chain economics question with a design-cycle component. Here are the forces pulling in each direction, weighted by order of magnitude.
★★★ Strongest Force For Renewal
Weight: Very High

Embedded Platform Specification

Nike's elite racing shoes (Alphafly 3, Vaporfly 4, Streakfly 2) are engineered around specific ZOTEK PEBA properties — cell structure, density, energy return curve, temperature resilience. RunRepeat lab data shows ZoomX becoming only 17% firmer after 20 minutes in a freezer — a key marathon performance attribute. Switching supplier mid-platform would require complete shoe re-engineering, re-testing, elite athlete re-validation, and retail recertification. These cycles take 2-3 years. World records (Kiptum Chicago 2023) have been set in ZoomX shoes — the IP validation is effectively permanent.
★★★ Strong Force For Renewal
Weight: High

November 2025 Strategic Partnership Expansion

In November 2025 Zotefoams announced an expanded strategic partnership with Nike — not just an extension of existing supply. This included three joint commitments with multi-year capital expenditure: (1) new Footwear Innovation Hub in Busan, South Korea, (2) former Nike Asia GM Brandon Thomas appointed as Zotefoams MD for Asia, (3) new global innovation centre at Croydon to accelerate product development. Companies planning to terminate in 4 years do not do this.
★★ Force For Renewal
Weight: Medium-High

Vietnam Manufacturing Joint Venture

Zotefoams' $32m Vietnam plant (commissioning Q4 2026, ramping 2027-28) is positioned specifically to serve Nike's Asian footwear assembly base. Seoheung's $10m JV stake (17.5%) ties a Korean footwear supply-chain specialist into the asset — creating a three-way interdependence. The plant is designed around Nike's tonnage requirements. Nike's silence on the commissioning does not mean opposition; it means this is standard contract manufacturing for them.
★★ Force For Renewal
Weight: Medium

History of Extensions + Expansions

The contract has been extended or expanded multiple times since 2017: (a) 2017 original signing, (b) 2022 extension into trail running (Zegama), (c) 2023 extension into basketball (GT Cut), (d) 2023 extension to Dec 2029, (e) 2023 Ultrafly programme, (f) 2025 strategic partnership expansion. That's a 9-year track record of deepening commitment, not cooling. Companies don't repeatedly expand relationships they're planning to exit.
★★ Strongest Force Against Renewal
Weight: Medium-High

A-TPU Technology Overtaking Pebax

The most concrete threat. As of 2025 lab testing, Aliphatic TPU foams have exceeded Pebax on energy return. ASICS' new FF Leap (2025), Saucony's PWRRUN HG, PUMA's Nitro Elite and — most importantly — Adidas' Lightstrike Pro Evo (used in Adios Pro Evo 2) are all A-TPU or A-TPU variants. RunRepeat: "A-TPU materials are now outperforming Pebax and hitting 80% in our lab test." Nike has no A-TPU capability through Zotefoams. If A-TPU becomes the sport standard, Nike must either (a) license A-TPU separately, (b) force Zotefoams to develop it, or (c) walk away.
★★ Force Against Renewal
Weight: Medium

Nike's "Win Now" Strategy + New CEO

Elliott Hill took over as Nike CEO in October 2024 and Matthew Friend (CFO) has explicitly flagged the need to "manage headwinds from repositioning" and "rebalance the portfolio." Nike's FY25 revenues were down 10% to $46.3bn, with gross margin compressing 14%. Chief Supply Chain Officer Venkatesh Alagirisamy was promoted to COO in Dec 2025 with a remit that explicitly includes manufacturing renegotiation. Every supplier contract is being pressure-tested. Zotefoams is not special in this regard.
★ Force Against Renewal
Weight: Low-Medium

Exclusivity Is Nike's Concession, Not ZTF's

Under the current deal Zotefoams cannot supply ANY other footwear brand (except medical). This is worth billions to Nike competitively — Saucony / PUMA / ASICS all use Pebax but must source from Arkema through different processors. In renewal negotiations, Nike's most obvious lever is "narrow the exclusivity." A "footwear cushioning — athletic running only" carve-out would let Nike keep ZTF's top-tier product while allowing ZTF to monetise basketball, trail, tennis supply to others. Commercial outcome: lower per-unit price to Nike in return for broader ZTF customer base.
★ Force Against Renewal
Weight: Low

Nike Has In-House Foam Capability

Nike owns NIKE IHM, Inc. (Missouri) which manufactures Air-Sole cushioning components in-house. Nike also has an ongoing supply-chain vertical-integration partnership with Apollo Global Management (2016). Technically, Nike could build its own Pebax processing line — but supercritical autoclave know-how has been compounding inside Croydon for 100+ years and the capital + time to replicate it is 3-5 years. For a 4-year contract window, building in-house does not beat renewing. For a 10-year window, it might.

Strategic Read
The competitive foam landscape has bifurcated in 2024-25. ZTF's ZoomX is still best-in-class for cold-weather stability and Nike's specific formulation, but A-TPU alternatives from BASF (Adidas), proprietary ASICS/PUMA/Saucony formulations now match or exceed on pure energy return. Nike's problem is not that ZoomX is bad — it's that the frontier has moved and ZTF's technology moat is narrower than it was in 2018. This is the single biggest argument for renegotiated rather than extended-as-is renewal.

For a position sized to handle the tail (1-2% of a portfolio), the expected value is genuinely attractive.

§ 7 — Conclusion

The Honest Probabilistic Answer

What to actually believe
Joe's Answer

Nike is very likely to renew — but probably not on the current terms. Base case ~65% is renewal with narrower exclusivity. The evidence base for this is strong: the November 2025 strategic partnership expansion was a multi-site capital commitment with joint talent transfer that makes no sense if Nike is planning to exit in 44 months. The Busan Innovation Hub and the Vietnam plant are 10-year assets, not 4-year bridges.

The real risk is not termination — it's commercial renegotiation. A-TPU has overtaken Pebax on energy return in 2025 lab tests, and Nike's new CEO and new COO are both focused on supply chain optimization. The likely 2028-2029 announcement will be "renewal on modified terms" — Nike takes lower pricing or carves out exclusivity in exchange for continued volume commitment.

The tail matters though. 20% non-renewal probability is not nothing. It's the reason ZTF trades at 9.5× EV/EBITDA rather than the 13-14× multiple its ROCE, margin trajectory and moat would otherwise command. If you can stomach the tail, you're being paid to hold it — the probability-weighted expected return is ~13% CAGR at current prices, which beats a risk-free alternative handily.

Watch for three early warning signs between now and 2027:

① Delay in Vietnam commissioning beyond Q4 2026 — would suggest Nike has slowed tonnage planning.
② Nike launches a major shoe platform using non-ZoomX foam — watch Vaporfly 5 closely.
③ No renewal announcement by end-2028 — would put ZTF firmly in bear scenario pricing.

If none of those three trigger by late 2028, you can treat the renewal risk as effectively resolved. Until then, size positions assuming the 20% tail is real and collect the compounding if it doesn't trigger.

One final thought — the risk framework above is what a careful analyst uses. What a cynical analyst would note is that Zotefoams' 2029 contract was extended in June 2023, 78 months before expiry. If that pattern holds, the next extension announcement will come sometime between Q3 2026 and Q2 2027. We should have an answer on renewal risk within 12-18 months — not 4 years. Watch the RNS feed.
